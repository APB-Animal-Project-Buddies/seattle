# Vegan Restaurant Recipe Catalog

> **Format:** publication-ready markdown reference document for a vegan-recipe website, structured through a restaurant operator's lens.
> **Scope:** 17 cuisines, **135 recipes**.
> **Currency / context:** US foodservice, 2025–2026 pricing.

---

## Tag Reference

Every recipe in this catalog carries a combination of the markers below.

**Course markers** (appear in the recipe title in bold):
- **STARTER** — appetizer, cold or warm; usually plated upscale.
- **MAIN** — entrée.
- **SHOWSTOPPER** — high-ticket destination dish that drives press, social, and check average. Typically $20–58.
- **DESSERT** — sweet course.

If no course marker appears in the title, the recipe is a standard main or all-day item for its cuisine.

**URL-status markers** (appear after the URL):
- **✓ VERIFIED** — URL was loaded against the live page and confirmed to host a fully-vegan recipe matching the listed dish. Use as-is.
- **⚠ REFERENCE (vegan)** — URL hosts a vegan recipe used as a *technique base* for a substitute-protein rebuild (e.g., a "vegan beef stew" base for the catalog's "Bò Kho with Chunk" rebuild). Operator should follow the linked recipe's method and substitute the listed protein.
- **⚠ REFERENCE (non-vegan / technique)** — URL is a traditional non-vegan technique tutorial; the catalog dish is a from-scratch vegan rebuild. **No published vegan recipe currently corresponds to the catalog entry.** Treat the substitution table as the operative recipe.

**Operational tags** (appear on the `Tags:` line of each recipe):
- **🥘 Bulk-Prep Friendly** — can be made in 10+ portion batches and held / reheated, lowering per-ticket labor.
- **⚡ Fast-Service Friendly** — plates in ≤8 minutes from prepped components, suitable for the lunch/express tier.

**Sourcing tier markers** (used in the Sourcing Tier Index below):
- **🌿 100% In-House** — recipe runs on tofu, seitan, tempeh, TVP, jackfruit, mushrooms, beans, lentils, cashews, or vegetables. No branded faux-meat required.
- **🥩 Alternative Meat** — recipe is built around a specific branded plant-meat product (Chunk Foods, Juicy Marbles, Beyond, Impossible, Gardein, Field Roast) as the default.
- **🌿/🥩 Two-Tier Hybrid** — recipe is structured with both a budget in-house tier and a premium branded tier on the same menu line, with two price points.

**Pricing tier ratios** (food-cost ÷ menu-price ratios used throughout):
- Fast-food: cost ÷ 0.32–0.35
- Fast-casual: cost ÷ 0.30–0.32
- Casual: cost ÷ 0.28–0.30
- Upscale: cost ÷ 0.25–0.27
- Bulk-prep dishes intentionally run hotter at 8–18% food cost.

## 🌿 Sourcing Tier Index — In-House vs Alternative Meat

This catalog can be operated at two distinct sourcing tiers. The classification below tells you which dishes work without buying any branded faux-meat product (Chunk Foods, Juicy Marbles, Beyond Meat, Impossible Foods, Gardein, Field Roast, etc.) and which dishes are built around a specific branded product as a signature ingredient.

**🌿 100% In-House** (~124 recipes / **~92% of catalog**) — built entirely from pantry staples and from-scratch proteins (tofu, seitan, tempeh, TVP, jackfruit, mushrooms, beans, lentils, cashews, vegetables). No branded faux-meat procurement required as default. Some have branded products listed as optional sub-table upgrades, but the verified default recipe runs without them. Lower COGS, fewer SKUs in the walk-in, full kitchen control — ideal for a small, scratch-focused independent operation.

**🥩 Alternative Meat** (9 recipes — almost entirely showstoppers) — built around a branded plant-meat product as the default. The dish identity, marketing, or essential character depends on that product.

**🌿/🥩 Two-Tier Hybrid** (2 fast-food recipes) — explicitly structured as both a budget tier (in-house) AND an upsell tier (branded) on the same menu line, with two price points.

**Operator takeaway:** A scratch-only kitchen can run **virtually the entire 135-recipe catalog** without ever opening a Chunk, JM, Beyond, or Impossible box — they only forfeit the ~9 high-ticket showstopper anchors ($24–58 tickets). A mixed kitchen carrying just three branded SKUs (Chunk Steakhouse Cut + Juicy Marbles Loin + Beyond Sausage) unlocks all 9 showstoppers plus the fast-food upsell tiers.

### 🌿 100% In-House (~124 recipes — works without branded faux-meat)

Every other recipe in the catalog runs on **tofu, homemade seitan, tempeh, TVP, jackfruit, whole mushrooms, beans, lentils, cashews, or vegetables**. This includes:

- **All 19 desserts** (no protein component, or naturally vegan) — including all three Gauthier French desserts (crème brûlée, iles flottantes, crème caramel).
- **Every Indian, French, Mexican, Japanese, Chinese, Middle Eastern, Mediterranean, Korean, and Spanish dish** in the catalog. These cuisines have such strong vegetarian/vegan-traditional foundations that branded plant-meats are almost never needed.
- **All 9 Chef Alexis Gauthier (Michelin-starred) French recipes** — faux gras (cashew-based), soupe VGE, soupe de poisson, barbajuans, bourguignon (default), coq au vin (default), and the three desserts. The Gauthier program is entirely buildable from cashews, mushrooms, sea vegetables, and pantry staples.
- **Most Italian, Ethiopian, Thai, Vietnamese, Caribbean, and Brazilian dishes** — the only exceptions are the ~9 branded items above.

### Table C — Menu Pricing Methodology

All "Suggested menu price" ranges below use the food cost % method:

- **Fast-casual** ($10–15 entrées) → food cost ÷ 0.32 ≈ menu price (range = food cost ÷ 0.35 to ÷ 0.30)
- **Casual sit-down** ($15–22 entrées) → ÷ 0.30
- **Upscale / steakhouse** ($24–45+) → ÷ 0.27
- **Sides / appetizers / soups** sized to category norms (cup of soup $7–10; side $5–8; appetizer $10–14)

### Vegan Crème Caramel — Gauthier (**DESSERT**)
- **URL:** https://chefalexisgauthier.substack.com/p/vegan-creme-caramel ✓ VERIFIED *(Chef Alexis Gauthier — Oct 2025. The classic French baked-version crème caramel (also called "flan parisien" / crème renversée), reimagined with soya milk + oat milk + cornflour + custard powder + coconut oil, set on top of an amber sugar caramel and bain-marie-baked, then unmoulded onto a plate so the caramel cascades over the inverted custard. **A second, distinct French dessert from Crème Brûlée** — not the same recipe, not the same texture: brûlée is set-in-ramekin with a shatter-crust top; caramel is unmoulded with a flowing caramel sauce.   **Recipe** (6-8 ramekins): **Caramel:** 120 g sugar + 3 tbsp water; cook in heavy saucepan over medium heat WITHOUT stirring until amber, immediately pour into the base of 6-8 ramekins, tilt to coat the bottom evenly, set aside to harden. **Custard:** 415 ml soya milk, 400 ml oat milk, 110 g sugar, 57 g cornflour, 23 g custard powder (or +23 g extra cornflour if unavailable), 70 g refined coconut oil (neutral flavour), 1 tbsp vanilla extract or seeds from 1 vanilla pod. Warm soya/oat milk + sugar + coconut oil gently to melt the oil and dissolve sugar (don't boil). Whisk cornflour + custard powder with a ladle of warm milk to make a smooth slurry. Whisk slurry into warm milk. Cook on medium 3-4 min, whisking, until just starting to thicken (still pourable, not pudding-like). Whisk in vanilla. **Strain through a fine sieve** to remove any lumps or skin. **Bake:** preheat 160°C (fan 140°C). Pour custard over caramel in ramekins. Place ramekins in deep roasting tray, pour hot water in tray to halfway up the sides (bain-marie). Bake 35-45 min until just set with slight wobble in centre. **Chill** to room temp, refrigerate **at least 4 hours, preferably overnight**. **Unmould:** run thin knife around edge, dip base briefly in hot water, invert onto plate. Caramel flows out and coats the custard.
